AIR QUALITY MONITORING SYSTEM:

AQMS can be used to monitor a wide range of air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM2.5) and Carbon Dioxide. Hence, enhancing air quality in urban environments, particularly in densely populated areas like Manila which is the Capital of the Philippines, is a critical challenge for public health and environmental sustainability.

To overcome this problem, a comprehensive smart air pollution monitoring system and smog prediction model can be vital in enhancing air quality in areas of Manila specifically in Quiapo, España Boulevard, and Intramuros.

The measure of how much water vapor is present in the air. It is expressed as a percentage of the maximum amount of water vapor that the air can hold at a given temperature. Too much humidity can make it difficult to breathe, and it can also lead to the growth of mold and mildew. Too little humidity can make the air feel dry and can irritate the skin and respiratory system.

Carbon dioxide (CO2) is a gas that is released into the atmosphere when we burn fossil fuels, such as coal, oil, and natural gas. It is also a byproduct of respiration. CO2 is a greenhouse gas, which means that it traps heat in the atmosphere. This is one of the main causes of climate change.

PM2.5, also known as fine particulate matter, is a type of air pollution that consists of tiny particles that are 2.5 micrometers in diameter or smaller. These particles are so small that they can be inhaled deep into the lungs, where they can cause a number of health problems.
